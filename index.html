Food quality in the U.S. looks normal — until you compare it.

This digital zine compares how major food groups are processed, labeled, and regulated in the United States versus other countries. The goal: show how standards shape what ends up on our plates.

Thesis

Food quality in the United States is lower than many other countries when evaluated across major food groups. Differences appear in processing levels and nutritional standards in foods such as grains, dairy, and produce. These disparities matter because food quality directly affects public health and long-term dietary outcomes.
